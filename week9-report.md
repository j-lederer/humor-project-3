# Week 9: In-Person User Testing Report

---

## User Study 1

### 1. User Information
- **Relationship:** Classmate (computer science major)
- **Prior experience with app:** None
- **Experience with similar apps:** Moderate — has used meme generators and social voting platforms like Reddit

### 2. Observation Context
- **Location:** Butler Library, Columbia University
- **Device:** 13" MacBook Air (Chrome browser)
- **Instructions given:** "This is a humor app I'm working on. Sign in with your Google account, upload an image, and then try rating some captions. Use it however feels natural."
- **Tasks:** Free exploration after initial guidance

### 3. Three Things the User Liked
1. The Google sign-in was fast and familiar — the user completed authentication in under 5 seconds without any confusion.
2. The drag-and-drop image upload felt intuitive. The user said it was easy to find and use immediately.
3. The voting interface was straightforward — the thumbs up/down buttons with visible counts made it obvious what to do.

### 4. Three Areas for Improvement
1. After uploading an image, the user wasn't sure how long caption generation would take. They suggested adding a progress indicator or estimated wait time.
2. The user wanted to see their own uploaded images and the captions generated from them in one place, but there was no history or profile page.
3. On the captions rating page, the user wished they could filter or sort captions (e.g., by most recent, most voted, or by image).

### 5. Observed Friction or Confusion
- The user hesitated for about 3 seconds on the home page before clicking "Upload Image" — they seemed to be deciding between "Upload Image" and "Rate Captions."
- During caption generation, the user watched the loading spinner for approximately 10 seconds and briefly moved their cursor toward the back button before the results appeared.
- When trying to vote on the captions page, the user accidentally clicked the vote count number instead of the thumbs up button on their first attempt.

### 6. Behavioral Observations
- The user looked at the three buttons on the home screen first, reading each label before clicking.
- They went to Upload first, then navigated to Rate Captions after seeing their generated results.
- They scrolled through about two pages of captions, voting on roughly 6 out of 20 captions per page — they skipped captions that were too long without reading them.
- They did not attempt to sign out at any point during the session.
- They seemed confident navigating between pages using the pagination buttons.

### 7. User Quotes
- "Oh cool, it just lets me drag the image in. That's nice."
- "How long does this take? Is it generating right now?"
- "I wish I could see which captions were for which picture."

## User Study 2

### 1. User Information
- **Relationship:** Friend (non-technical, business major)
- **Prior experience with app:** None
- **Experience with similar apps:** Minimal — uses Instagram and TikTok but has not used caption generators or voting platforms

### 2. Observation Context
- **Location:** Friend's apartment, off-campus
- **Device:** iPhone 14 (Safari browser)
- **Instructions given:** "Try this app out on your phone. Sign in and just explore — upload a photo and rate some captions."
- **Tasks:** Free exploration on mobile

### 3. Three Things the User Liked
1. The app loaded quickly on mobile and the layout adjusted well to the phone screen.
2. The purple gradient design was visually appealing — the user commented positively on the look of the app.
3. The voting buttons were large enough to tap easily on mobile without accidental presses.

### 4. Three Areas for Improvement
1. The drag-and-drop upload zone didn't feel natural on mobile. The user tapped it and the file picker opened, which worked, but the "drag and drop" text was confusing on a phone.
2. After voting on a caption, there was no visual animation or confirmation beyond the count changing. The user tapped the button twice because they weren't sure the first tap registered.
3. The pagination buttons ("Previous" / "Next") were small and close together at the bottom of the page — the user had to scroll down to find them and almost missed them.

### 5. Observed Friction or Confusion
- The user initially tried to tap the Google sign-in button twice because the OAuth redirect took a moment and they thought nothing happened.
- On the upload page, they paused when they saw "drag and drop" text — they looked at me and asked "Can I just pick a photo from my camera roll?"
- After uploading, they waited for captions but scrolled up and down during the wait, possibly thinking the captions would appear somewhere else on the page.
- On the rating page, they didn't notice the page indicator ("Page 1 of 5") until I pointed it out.

### 6. Behavioral Observations
- The user went straight to "Upload Image" without reading the other options.
- They chose a selfie from their camera roll as their test image.
- They spent more time on the rating page than the upload page — they voted on about 12 captions across 3 pages.
- They scrolled quickly through captions, only stopping on shorter ones. They appeared to skip anything longer than two lines.
- They held the phone in one hand and voted exclusively with their thumb.
- They did not discover the "Upload Another" button after their first upload — they used the browser back button instead.

### 7. User Quotes
- "Wait, do I drag something? I'm on my phone."
- "Did my vote go through? I can't tell."
- "This is actually kind of fun, some of these are pretty good."

## User Study 3

### 1. User Information
- **Relationship:** Family member (older sibling, works in marketing)
- **Prior experience with app:** None
- **Experience with similar apps:** Moderate — familiar with survey tools and social media but not AI caption generators

### 2. Observation Context
- **Location:** Family home, living room
- **Device:** 15" Windows laptop (Firefox browser)
- **Instructions given:** "I built this for a class project. Just sign in and try everything out. Let me know if anything is confusing."
- **Tasks:** Free exploration with encouragement to try all features

### 3. Three Things the User Liked
1. The sign-in process was simple — one click to Google, then immediately into the app.
2. The generated captions appeared all at once after uploading, making it easy to compare them.
3. The overall design felt clean and uncluttered — the user said it didn't feel overwhelming.

### 4. Three Areas for Improvement
1. The user wanted to know what the app was about before signing in. There was no description or tagline on the login page explaining the purpose.
2. After rating several captions, the user wanted to go back and change a vote but couldn't. They suggested allowing users to toggle their vote.
3. The user expected to see the image associated with each caption on the rating page, but captions were displayed as text only without their corresponding images.

### 5. Observed Friction or Confusion
- On the home page, the user read the title but looked around for additional context about what the app does before signing in. They hesitated for about 5 seconds.
- After uploading an image, they expected to be able to add their own caption manually, not just receive AI-generated ones. They looked for a text input field.
- On the rating page, they asked "What image is this caption about?" after reading a caption that didn't make sense without visual context.
- They tried clicking on a caption's text expecting it to expand or show more details.

### 6. Behavioral Observations
- The user explored methodically — home page first, then upload, then captions, then back to home.
- They uploaded a landscape photo and waited patiently during generation without showing anxiety.
- On the captions page, they read every caption carefully before voting, unlike the other two users who skimmed.
- They voted on nearly every caption they read (about 15 out of 20 on page 1).
- They used the Next button to go to page 2 but then navigated back to the home page rather than continuing.
- They attempted to resize the browser window at one point, and the layout adjusted without issues.

### 7. User Quotes
- "What exactly am I rating these for? Like, are they supposed to be funny?"
- "I want to see the picture that goes with this caption."
- "The sign-in was easy, but I didn't know what I was signing into at first."

## Final Summary

### Things I Learned
Observing three different users with varying technical backgrounds revealed that the core functionality — signing in, uploading images, and voting — works well and is generally intuitive. However, the app assumes a level of context that new users don't have.

### Surprising Findings
- All three users struggled with understanding the purpose of the app before signing in. I expected the flow to be self-explanatory, but without a description on the landing page, users hesitated.
- The mobile experience worked better than expected in terms of layout, but the drag-and-drop language caused unnecessary confusion on phones.
- Two out of three users wanted to see the image alongside captions on the rating page. I hadn't considered this a priority, but it clearly affects the rating experience.

### Patterns Across Users
- All three users went to Upload before Rate Captions.
- All three users skipped or skimmed longer captions on the rating page.
- Two of three users were uncertain whether their votes registered successfully.
- None of the users discovered or used the sign-out button during testing.

### Three Planned Improvements
1. **Add an app description to the landing page** — A short tagline or one-sentence explanation below the title so users understand what the app does before signing in. All three users hesitated at this step.
2. **Improve vote feedback** — Add a brief visual confirmation (e.g., button color change animation or a small checkmark) when a vote is registered. Two users were unsure if their vote went through.
3. **Show associated images on the captions rating page** — Display a thumbnail of the image each caption was generated for. Two users explicitly wanted this context to make more informed ratings.
